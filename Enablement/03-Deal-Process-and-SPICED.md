# Deal Process and SPICED Framework

**Audience:** Sellers, Sales Managers | **Topics:** SPICED, pipeline, stages, exit criteria, qualification, forecast | **Last Updated:** May 2026

---

## SPICED Framework Overview

SPICED is the structured methodology Knotch uses to document opportunity qualification and manage deal progression. Each letter represents a critical dimension of the deal:

- **S**ituation
- **P**ain
- **I**mpact
- **C**ritical Event
- **E**Decision Criteria

SPICED ensures every deal has documented evidence of customer problem fit, business urgency, and clear evaluation framework before advancing through pipeline stages.

---

## SPICED Elements Defined

### Situation (S)

**Definition:** What is currently happening at the customer company? What is the business context that makes them receptive to a solution like Knotch?

**What to capture:**

- Current state of their content marketing program (maturity, structure, budget)
- Digital transformation initiatives or channel shifts
- Recent organizational changes (leadership, team structure, tools consolidation)
- Current analytics/measurement approach and gaps
- Ownership of content function (marketing, brand, performance marketing, separate team?)

**Examples:**

- "Tech SaaS company with 8-person content team producing 30+ pieces/month across owned and paid channels; currently using 4 disparate tools (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Facebook Analytics, Mixpanel) to track performance with no unified view."
- "CPG brand investing $2M+ annually in content marketing for owned channels; recently hired new VP of Digital who is consolidating vendor stack and wants single source of truth for ROI measurement."
- "Financial services firm expanding content into thought leadership; Chief Marketing Officer wants to move from vanity metrics (page views) to business impact metrics (pipeline influenced, revenue attributed)."

**Format:** 2–4 sentences, conversational; capture the customer's worldview and current operating model.

---

### Pain (P)

**Definition:** What specific problem or operational challenge does the customer face? What is broken, inefficient, or unmeasurable?

**What to capture:**

- Measurement gap (what they can't measure or see)
- Attribution blindness (which content drives pipeline and revenue)
- Tool fragmentation (too many platforms, poor data integration)
- Resource inefficiency (manual processes, time spent on analytics)
- Credibility challenge (can't justify content spend to finance/executive team)
- Performance opacity (don't know which content is working)

**Examples:**

- "Can't attribute revenue to content; no way to tell if $500K/year content investment is generating pipeline or just impressions."
- "Marketing team spends 15+ hours/week manually pulling data from multiple platforms to create executive reports; process is error-prone and misses cross-channel attribution."
- "Digital media spend is growing but CMO lacks proof that owned content complements paid campaigns; no visibility into halo effect or synergies."
- "Agency produced 50+ pieces of content last year; company has no systematic way to measure engagement or performance, so renewal decision is subjective."

**Format:** 1–3 sentences per pain point; be specific and quantified where possible.

---

### Impact (I)

**Definition:** What is the business consequence if this pain remains unsolved? What does it cost the customer to stay in this situation?

**What to capture:**

- Wasted marketing spend (money spent without ROI visibility)
- Missed revenue opportunities (potential pipeline left on the table)
- Executive credibility risk (CMO or content leader at risk if can't prove value)
- Competitive disadvantage (competitors are measuring, optimizing; this company is not)
- Process burden (FTE hours wasted on manual analytics)
- Decision quality (making content strategy decisions blind)

**Examples:**

- "If CMO can't prove content ROI, executive team will cut content budget by 20% despite strong anecdotal evidence of impact; direct revenue loss of $100K+ in annual content investment and potential customer lifetime value impact."
- "Without unified measurement, company continues to over-invest in low-performing content types and under-invest in high-performers; estimated 15–20% efficiency loss across $2M content budget ($300–400K annually)."
- "VP of Digital inherited fragmented measurement approach; lack of clean reporting puts credibility at risk in first-year goals review."
- "Competitors are optimizing content based on attribution data; this company is producing content by instinct and tradition, risking relevance and engagement decline."

**Format:** 1–2 sentences; quantify impact where possible (dollars, percentage, FTE hours).

---

### Critical Event (E)

**Definition:** What is the deadline, trigger, or forcing function that makes this decision urgent? Why are they talking to you now?

**What to capture:**

- Budget cycle (new fiscal year planning, budget allocation)
- Executive mandate (new leader, new strategic goal)
- Contract event (vendor renewal, tool consolidation decision)
- Organizational change (team restructure, M&A, leadership change)
- Competitive pressure (losing deals, customer feedback)
- Existing tool expiration (SaaS renewal, EOL decision)
- Board/investor requirement (quarterly results, strategic review)

**Examples:**

- "Fiscal year 2026 budget planning cycle starts in Q2; VP of Marketing needs to justify content team headcount and tool spend; decision required by April 15."
- "New Chief Marketing Officer joined 60 days ago with mandate to 'optimize marketing spend and prove ROI'; vendor stack review scheduled for March."
- "Contract with current analytics platform renews in 90 days at 40% price increase; brand considering alternatives."
- "Board of Directors asked management to improve content ROI metrics in next quarterly earnings call (8 weeks)."
- "Company just completed agency RFP; winning agency (selected 2 weeks ago) requires unified measurement platform to manage performance."

**Format:** 1 sentence; be specific about date/timeline.

---

### Decision Criteria (D)

**Definition:** How will the customer evaluate solutions? What are the must-haves, nice-to-haves, and evaluation framework?

**What to capture:**

- Technical requirements (integrations, data architecture, API, reporting)
- Business requirements (ROI proof, ease of use, time-to-value)
- Organizational criteria (team training, adoption, change management)
- Financial criteria (budget, ROI payback, acceptable price point)
- Competitive comparison (how they'll benchmark Knotch against alternatives)
- Success metrics (what does "good" look like after implementation?)
- Approval criteria (who needs to sign off, legal/compliance requirements)

**Examples:**

- "Must integrate with existing GA4 and HubSpot; proof-of-concept must deliver sample ROI report in 2 weeks; pricing must be <$150K annually; CFO approval required."
- "Must work with existing content management system (WordPress) and paid social platforms (Facebook, LinkedIn); 60-day implementation; easy reporting for non-technical CMO; <$100K price point."
- "Solution must provide weekly dashboard updates for executive team; ability to attribute revenue across owned and paid channels; API to feed data back into CDP; budget approval from VP Finance and sign-off from Chief Marketing Officer."
- "Will evaluate us against Contentsquare and Littledata; must demo ROI calculation methodology; integration with Google Analytics 4 non-negotiable."

**Format:** 2–3 sentences; structure as must-haves, nice-to-haves, and evaluation framework.

## Deal Progression & Stage Exit Criteria

Deals progress through the New/Expansion pipeline by satisfying stage exit criteria. A deal cannot advance to the next stage until the exit criteria are met.

### Stage 1: IPM → Qualification (Stage 1)

**Exit Criteria (must-haves):**

- Meeting confirmed on calendar with at least one participant from prospect company
- Meeting held (completed)
- Meeting notes documented in deal record
- **Situation documented** – 2–3 sentence description of their current state and business context
- **Pain identified** – At least 1 specific pain point documented
- **Deal amount estimated** – Preliminary revenue estimate (can be range)
- **At least 1 contact identified** – Primary contact with name, title, and role noted

**Exit Criteria (nice-to-haves):**

- Buying group started (2+ contacts identified)
- Basic account tier assigned (Tier I, II, or III based on enrichment/ICP score)
- Champion identified (executive with strategic interest)

**Actions:**

- Confirm meeting date/time with prospect
- Add attendees on prospect side to buying group
- Document agenda in deal notes
- Send calendar confirmation with Zoom/Teams link
- Schedule follow-up meeting(s) to deepen pain understanding
- Add deal amount to HubSpot deal record
- Begin building buying group profile
- Update SPICED Situation and Pain fields

**Timeline:** 15–25 business days from IPM

---

### Stage 3: Qualification (Stage 1) → Consensus (Stage 2)

**Exit Criteria (must-haves):**

- **Full SPICED documented** – Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, and Decision Criteria all documented
- **Buying group identified** – 2+ contacts with named roles (title, function, level of influence)
- **Champion identified** – Primary internal advocate identified (usually director+ or C-level)
- **Technical requirements understood** – Integration, data architecture, or deployment questions answered at high level

**Exit Criteria (nice-to-haves):**

- Economic buyer engaged (conversation with finance stakeholder)
- Competitive situation assessed (aware of alternatives customer is considering)
- Proof of concept or pilot approach outlined

**Actions:**

- Conduct discovery calls with 2+ stakeholders from different departments/functions
- Document SPICED fields completely and in detail
- Update buying group and champion fields in deal record
- Identify next meeting or consensus-building activity
- Draft preliminary business case outline (internally; don't share yet)

**Timeline:** 20–30 business days from Stage 1 entry

---

### Stage 4: Consensus (Stage 2) → Proposal + Business Case (Stage 3)

**Exit Criteria (must-haves):**

- **Economic buyer engaged** – At least one conversation with procurement, finance, or budget owner
- **Decision criteria confirmed** – Customer has articulated evaluation criteria; AE has documented in SPICED
- **Business case drafted** – ROI case with assumptions, timeline, and value quantification prepared (internal working document)
- **3+ contacts mapped** – Buying group includes at least 3 named participants at appropriate levels

**Exit Criteria (nice-to-haves):**

- Competitive comparison underway (customer is evaluating alternatives and we know who)
- Executive sponsor identified (C-level stakeholder aware and supportive)
- Timeline for decision solidified

**Actions:**

- Conduct business case workshop with customer team (optional but recommended)
- Prepare formal proposal document aligned to decision criteria
- Draft ROI calculation with customer-specific assumptions
- Create executive summary (1-pager) for C-level stakeholder
- Schedule proposal delivery meeting
- Update deal close date with realistic estimate

**Timeline:** 15–20 business days from Stage 2 entry

---

### Stage 5: Proposal + Business Case (Stage 3) → Procurement (Stage 4)

**Exit Criteria (must-haves):**

- **Proposal delivered** – Formal proposal document (quote + SOW) delivered and reviewed
- **Pricing agreement** – Customer agrees to pricing or negotiation is underway with path to resolution
- **Legal/procurement contacts added** – Known procurement, legal, or IT contacts added to buying group
- **Executive alignment confirmed** – E-signature authority (CFO, CMO, CEO) confirmed as supportive

**Exit Criteria (nice-to-haves):**

- Contract negotiations initiated
- Internal approvals (legal, finance) from customer side underway
- Security questionnaire or SOC2 review started
- Vendor requirements assessment (background check, insurance, etc.) initiated

**Actions:**

- Deliver proposal in customer-appropriate format (slide deck, PDF document, or web portal)
- Walk through proposal with economic buyer and champion
- Address questions and objections
- Negotiate terms if needed (discount, payment schedule, contract length)
- Introduce procurement/legal contact from Knotch side
- Provide copy of standard SOW and T&Cs
- Schedule procurement kickoff meeting

**Timeline:** 10–15 business days from Stage 3 entry

---

### Stage 6: Procurement (Stage 4) → Closed Won

**Exit Criteria (must-haves):**

- **Contract executed** – All signatures completed (both parties signed)
- **Payment terms arranged** – Invoice schedule and payment method confirmed
- **Implementation timeline agreed** – Onboarding and go-live date confirmed with customer

**Exit Criteria (nice-to-haves):**

- Security review completed (SOC2, NDA, insurance verification)
- Internal onboarding task list created and assigned
- Executive kick-off meeting scheduled
- Customer success manager assigned

**Actions:**

- Manage contract negotiation and redlines
- Coordinate with Legal on final contract terms
- Obtain all required signatures (executive sign-off, authorized signers on both sides)
- Confirm invoice and payment details
- Schedule implementation kickoff meeting
- Hand off to customer success team
- Update deal status to Closed Won and record final contract value

**Timeline:** 10–45 business days depending on customer procurement process

## Smart Property Prompts for SPICED Fields

Use these prompts when documenting each SPICED element in HubSpot. Formatting guidance ensures consistency and clarity.

### Situation Field Prompt

_"Describe the customer's current state. What is their content marketing structure, team, and current measurement approach? What business initiatives are underway? Format: 2–4 sentences, conversational tone."_

**Good example:**
"Tech SaaS company with $8M in annual revenue, 6-person demand gen team. Currently runs content marketing across company blog, LinkedIn, and paid channels with ~$250K annual spend. Using Google Analytics 4 and HubSpot for reporting, but no unified view of content performance across owned + paid. Recently hired VP of Demand Gen (3 months ago) tasked with consolidating martech stack and improving efficiency."

**Weak example:**
"Large company with content. Uses analytics. Wants better data."

---

### Pain Field Prompt

_"What is the specific problem or gap? Be concrete and quantified. Format: 1–2 sentences per pain point; list multiple pains if they exist."_

**Good example:**
"Pain 1: No attribution from content to pipeline. VP of Demand Gen can't tell which blog topics, pieces, or channels are influencing deals. Makes it impossible to justify continued investment to finance team.
Pain 2: Manual reporting process takes 6–8 hours per week across team. Every executive request requires custom Sheets pull from multiple data sources; high error rate and delays."

**Weak example:**
"Analytics is hard."

---

### Impact Field Prompt

_"What happens if they don't solve this problem? What does it cost them? Format: Quantify in dollars, FTE hours, or percentage of spend."_

**Good example:**
"If VP can't demonstrate ROI, finance will cut content budget by 25% in next fiscal year planning (Q4 this year). That's $60K in lost annual spend and ~2 FTE reduced headcount. Additionally, without performance visibility, team continues to over-invest in low-performing content types; estimated 15–20% efficiency loss across $250K budget."

**Weak example:**
"Bad things will happen."

---

### Critical Event Field Prompt

_"What is the forcing function or deadline? What makes this decision urgent? Format: Specific date or timeline."_

**Good example:**
"Q4 budget planning cycle starts October 1 (45 days from now); VP must have justification for demand gen budget and team structure by October 15. Fiscal year 2025 commences January 1."

**Weak example:**
"They need it soon."

---

### Decision Criteria Field Prompt

_"How will they evaluate solutions? What are must-haves, nice-to-haves, and success metrics? Format: Structure as 'Must-have: ...', 'Nice-to-have: ...', 'Success metric: ...'."_

**Good example:**
"Must-have: Integrates with GA4 and HubSpot; delivers sample ROI analysis in 2-week pilot; pricing <$200K annually.
Nice-to-have: API to push data back to CDP; Slack integration for alerts.
Success metric: Executive dashboard reporting on content ROI with monthly refresh; attributable pipeline/revenue by content topic and channel; ROI payback in <18 months."

**Weak example:**
"Good tool, affordable."

## Account Planning Properties

Beyond SPICED, use these account-level properties to plan expansion and manage risk:

### Strategic Priorities

**Definition:** What is the customer trying to achieve in the next 12–24 months? Where are they investing?

**Examples:**

- "Expanding B2B content program; building thought leadership for new exec team; consolidating marketing tech stack"
- "Optimizing paid social ROI; moving from agency-produced to in-house content"
- "Proving marketing's contribution to revenue to c-suite; rebuilding analytics capability"

**Refresh:** Quarterly in QBRs or renewal conversations

### Competitive Landscape

**Definition:** Who are the competing vendors or alternative solutions the customer is using or considering?

**Examples:**

- "Currently using Contentsquare + GA4; also evaluating Littledata and in-house tagging solution"
- "Considering both Knotch and competitive vendor X; both in evaluation phase"
- "Using internal attribution model; open to third-party tool but no active process"

**Refresh:** Document during deal; update on renewal

### Expansion Opportunities

**Definition:** Which additional modules, capabilities, or use cases could the customer use?

**Examples:**

- "ACE (content generation) – customer producing 40+ pieces/year manually; could benefit from AI drafting"
- "Content Catalogs – customer building new vertical; could use pre-built content templates"
- "Enrichment (AIQ/EQ) – customer has buyer intelligence need; could expand from measurement to intelligence"

**Refresh:** Annually; during quarterly business reviews with customers

### Account Risk Flag

**Definition:** Assessment of churn or loss risk. Used to prioritize customer success and expansion activities.

**Values:**

- **Green** – Low risk; customer engaged, healthy metrics, no known issues
- **Yellow** – Moderate risk; customer has not adopted fully, soft signals of dissatisfaction, or upcoming contract challenge
- **Red** – High risk; customer dissatisfied, using product minimally, or contract renewal at risk

**Refresh:** Monthly during forecast calls; customer success drives updates

## Buying Group Blueprint

Map stakeholders to pipeline stage. This guides outreach and buying group composition.

### IPM

**Minimum contacts:** 1–2
**Typical roles:** Scheduler contact (often coordinator or junior marketing stakeholder)
**Activity:** Calendar confirmation, first meeting

### Qualification (Stage 1)

**Minimum contacts:** 2–3
**Typical roles:**

- Primary stakeholder (director+ in content/marketing)
- Secondary (peer from digital or performance marketing)
  **Activity:** Discovery and pain validation

### Consensus (Stage 2)

**Minimum contacts:** 3–5
**Typical roles:**

- Content leader (director+)
- Digital or performance marketing peer
- Finance or budget stakeholder (optional but valuable)
- Executive sponsor (VP+ with strategic interest)
  **Activity:** Solution fit assessment, champion identification

### Proposal + Business Case (Stage 3)

**Minimum contacts:** 4–6
**Typical roles:**

- Content leader
- Digital/performance marketing peer
- Economic buyer (VP, CMO, or CFO with budget authority)
- Executive sponsor
- IT/technical stakeholder (if technical integration questions remain)
  **Activity:** Business case walkthrough, proposal review

### Procurement (Stage 4)

**Minimum contacts:** 5+
**Typical roles:**

- All previous stakeholders plus:
- Legal contact (procurement, contracts)
- IT security or compliance (if applicable)
- Internal contract signer (CFO, VP Finance, CMO)
  **Activity:** Contract negotiation, vendor onboarding

## Sales Cycle Timeline Expectations

**Typical New Business Deal Timeline:**

- **IPM to Qualification:** 15–20 business days (IPM + 1–2 follow-ups)
- **Qualification to Consensus:** 20–30 business days (multiple stakeholder conversations, internal champion building)
- **Consensus to Proposal:** 15–20 business days (business case prep, proposal drafting, internal review)
- **Proposal to Procurement:** 10–15 business days (pricing negotiation, legal initiation)
- **Procurement to Close:** 10–45 business days (contract negotiation, internal approvals, signature cycle)

**Total: 70–150 business days (3.5–7.5 months)** from IPM to Close Won

**Factors that extend cycle:**

- Large deal size (requires more stakeholder alignment)
- Customer procurement process (some companies have 6–8 week review cycles)
- Executive approval layers (higher ARR often requires C-level sign-off)
- Competitive evaluation (customer is evaluating multiple vendors)
- Budget cycle timing (deal delayed until new fiscal year)

**Factors that accelerate cycle:**

- Clear champion and executive sponsor
- Fast critical event (imminent budget deadline or contract renewal)
- Smaller deal size (<$150K)
- Existing relationship or net-new-to-market vendor (less procurement friction)

## Deal Documentation Best Practices

### Naming Convention

Use format: `[Company Name] - [Product/Use Case] - [FY]`

**Good examples:**

- "Acme Corp - Measurement Platform - FY25"
- "TechCorp Inc - Content Intelligence Expansion - FY25"
- "BigRetail LLC - Owned Channel Analytics - FY26"

### Notes & Record Keeping

- Document each conversation in deal timeline (date, attendees, summary)
- Update SPICED fields after each discovery conversation (don't wait until stage exit)
- Link relevant emails, call recordings, or proposals to deal record
- Use deal tags to categorize (e.g., "Expansion," "Competitive," "High-Risk," "Fast-Track")

### Forecast Updates

- Update forecast category when deal advances stages
- Update close date estimate after customer meetings (don't assume original estimate)
- Document win/loss reason on close (required for closed deals)
