## Getting Started

### Q: What CRM does Knotch use?

**A:** HubSpot (Portal ID: 44523005). All deal, contact, and company data lives in HubSpot. It's your single source of truth. If it's not in HubSpot, it didn't happen—leadership coaches and forecasts from CRM data.

### Q: What is Knotch's product?

**A:** Knotch is a content intelligence platform that helps brands measure content marketing ROI across owned and paid channels. We provide data-driven insights on what content performs, why, and how it impacts business outcomes.

**Key Products:**

- **Knotch One:** The flagship analytics platform
- **ACE (Agentic Content Engine):** AI-powered content operations automation
- **K1:** Content scoring and analytics
- **Content Catalysts:** Professional services and implementation
- **JCI:** Joint Content Intelligence offering
- **KSC:** Knotch Service Cloud
- **AIQ:** AI-powered insights and analytics
- **EQ/IQ:** Engagement and Impact quotients

### Q: Who is our Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)?

**A:** Enterprise and upper-mid-market brands with significant content and digital marketing investments. Specifically:

- Companies with **dedicated content teams** (e.g., Director of Content, Content Managers)
- Companies with **marketing leadership** and strategic marketing investment (CMOs, VP Marketing, Digital Directors)
- Organizations with **significant paid media spend** looking to measure ROI
- Brands managing **owned channels** (blogs, email, podcasts, webinars) and wanting attribution

Target account size: Usually $500M+ in annual revenue with sophisticated martech stacks.

### Q: What sales methodology does Knotch use?

**A:** **SPICED** — Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision Criteria.

Every deal should have SPICED fields populated as you progress through pipeline stages:

- **Situation:** What is the prospect's current state? (business context, current tools, team structure)
- **Pain:** What problems are they experiencing? (measurement gaps, content underperformance, attribution blindness)
- **Impact:** What's the business impact of not solving the pain? (revenue at risk, wasted marketing spend, missed opportunities)
- **Critical Event:** What event or deadline is driving urgency? (budget cycle, Q planning, new initiative launch, leadership change)
- **Decision Criteria:** What will they evaluate on? (price, implementation time, features, vendor stability, references)

Update SPICED fields as you learn more about the deal. Use them to guide discovery conversations and position value.

## Accounts & Territory

### Q: How are accounts tiered?

**A:** Accounts are segmented into three tiers based on enterprise value and strategic importance:

- **Tier I:** Largest enterprise accounts with highest win rates and largest average deal sizes. ~50-80 accounts
- **Tier II:** Best expansion economics with 3x LTV potential (expansion revenue significantly higher than land). ~300-400 accounts
- **Tier III:** Upper mid-market accounts with good unit economics but smaller initial land size. Remainder of target account universe

**Total Target Accounts:** 1,112

Each tier receives proportional attention and strategy. Tier I gets highest-touch early engagement; Tier II gets efficiency focus; Tier III gets volume focus.

### Q: How are accounts assigned to me?

**A:** Territory assignments are based on:

1. **Geography (80%):** Primary allocation is by geographic region/territory
2. **Tier Mix Balance:** Each rep receives a balanced mix of Tier I, II, and III accounts to optimize revenue potential
3. **Active Deal Protection:** If another rep is actively in a deal at an account you're assigned, you cannot cold-call that account until deal is closed

**Account Load:** Each AE typically gets ~275–300 target accounts to work.

### Q: What tools help me prioritize which accounts to work?

**A:** Prioritize accounts using these signals:

- **ICP Tier:** Account segmentation into Tier I (enterprise), Tier II (mid-market), or Tier III (upper mid-market)
- **Company Size:** Revenue and employee count; larger enterprises with sophisticated content and marketing operations are better fits
- **Technology Stack:** Companies with advanced MarTech investments signal marketing maturity and buying power
- **Recent Activity:** News, leadership changes, or marketing initiatives indicate readiness to evaluate solutions
- **Engagement Indicators:** Website visits, content downloads, or direct outreach responsiveness signal interest

**Action:** Start with Tier I accounts and accounts showing recent engagement. Research company news and initiatives to personalize outreach.

### Q: How many accounts have never been contacted?

**A:** **59% of accounts are uncontacted** (657 of 1,112 total accounts).

**Priority:** You should work your existing book of contacted accounts before asking for new territory. Uncontacted accounts are available as you exhaust existing pipeline opportunities or complete expansion cycles within active accounts.

## Deal Management

### Q: What are the pipeline stages?

**A:** Knotch's pipeline follows this sequence:

1. **IPM** — Initial Pipeline Meeting scheduled and held (first meeting, early conversation, relationship building)
2. **Qualification (Stage 1)** — Active discovery and qualification; confirm pain and fit
3. **Consensus (Stage 2)** — Buyer consensus around problem and Knotch as solution
4. **Proposal + Business Case (Stage 3)** — Formal proposal shared; business case being evaluated
5. **Procurement (Stage 4)** — Legal review, contracting, final approvals in progress
6. **Closed Won** — Deal closed and signed (revenue recognized)
7. **Closed Lost** — Deal lost; record reason for analysis

Deals move sequentially (no skipping stages). Typically 60-day sales cycle target.

### Q: What fields do I need to update on my deals every week?

**A:** Every Friday, update these fields for **every open deal**:

1. **Amount** — Deal size in USD
2. **Deal Stage** — Current stage (based on buyer progress, not wishful thinking)
3. **Forecast Category** — Omitted, At Risk, Commit, Upside (honest assessment of close likelihood)
4. **Forecast Probability** — Overall % likelihood deal closes this quarter (drives forecast math)
5. **Next Step** — What happens next? (call scheduled, proposal being prepared, awaiting legal feedback)
6. **Next Step Date** — When will that next step occur? (must be in the future)
7. **Close Date** — Expected close date (used to stage pipeline)

**Effort:** Block 15 minutes on Friday to update all open deals. This is your #1 most important weekly activity.

### Q: What are deal hygiene tags and what do they mean?

**A:** Deal hygiene tags are **automated flags** that surface deals needing immediate action:

1. **No Amount** — Deal record has no amount filled in. Stage gate failure. Do not progress deal without amount.
2. **Stalled** — Deal has been in the same stage for 45+ days with no progression (momentum loss).
3. **Zombie** — Deal shows zero logged activity for 45+ days (at risk of being lost silently).
4. **No Recent Activity** — No activity logged in 21+ days (prospect engagement has stalled).
5. **No Contacts** — Deal has zero contacts assigned (you don't have a buyer to talk to).
6. **Single Threaded** — Only one contact assigned at Qualification stage or later (concentration risk if contact leaves).
7. **Stale Next Steps** — Next step date is in the past but deal is still open (you missed a commitment).
8. **Past-Due Close** — Close date has already passed but deal is still in open pipeline (forecast accuracy issue).

**Goal:** **Zero flags.** Every flag represents a deal quality issue that blocks close. Review your flags in the Deal Pipeline Hygiene dashboard weekly and resolve immediately.

### Q: Where do I see my hygiene flags?

**A:** **Reporting > Dashboards > Deal Pipeline Hygiene Dashboard** (HubSpot Portal 44523005, Dashboard ID: 19333613)

Use the quick filter to show only your deals. Every report breaks down hygiene by flag type. Spend 15 minutes Friday resolving all flags on your deals.

### Q: How many contacts should be assigned to a deal at each stage?

**A:** Minimum contact requirements by stage to reduce single-threaded risk:

- **IPM:** 1–2 contacts (usually one initial contact who invited you in)
- **Qualification (Stage 1):** 2–3 contacts (establish relationships with champion and at least one peer)
- **Consensus (Stage 2):** 3–5 contacts (broader stakeholder alignment; economic buyer, champion, and technical influencers)
- **Proposal + Business Case (Stage 3):** 4–6+ contacts (stakeholders evaluating proposal, procurement beginning)
- **Procurement (Stage 4):** 5+ contacts (executive sponsor, economic buyer, legal, procurement, end user)

**No single-threaded deals past Qualification.** If a deal reaches Consensus with only one contact, you've failed on discovery and account mapping.

### Q: What buying roles should I assign to contacts?

**A:** Assign one or more of these roles to every contact on a deal (based on their actual function in this deal):

1. **Decision Maker** — Has final authority to approve or reject deal
2. **Economic Buyer** — Controls budget; may not use the product but approves spend
3. **Champion** — Internally advocating for Knotch; has personal stake in win
4. **Executive Sponsor** — Senior leader providing political/strategic support
5. **Influencer** — Shapes opinion without final authority; often technical or functional expert
6. **Technical Buyer** — Evaluating product fit and implementation feasibility
7. **End User** — Will use the product day-to-day (if applicable; content teams, marketing ops)
8. **Blocker** — Has veto power (often Legal, IT, Procurement)
9. **Legal & Compliance** — Evaluating contract terms and data/privacy requirements
10. **Procurement** — Managing RFP process, vendor selection, contract execution
11. **AI Governance/Risk** — Evaluating AI use policy and risk implications (increasingly important)

Every contact should have at least one role. Most will have multiple. Roles guide your conversation strategy and stakeholder management.

## Personas & Buyers

### Q: What personas does Knotch sell to?

**A:** Knotch has **15 defined buyer personas** based on job function and buying power.

**Primary Target Personas:**

- **Content/Demand Gen Specialist or Manager** — Owns content creation and distribution; wants to prove ROI and optimize spend
- **CMO (Chief Marketing Officer) / VP Marketing** — Strategic leader accountable for marketing results and budget; wants attribution and efficiency
- **Digital Marketing Manager / Director** — Owns digital channels and campaign performance; needs measurement and optimization tools
- **Brand Manager / Director** — Owns brand content and messaging; wants to understand impact on brand perception and customer engagement
- **IT / Analytics / MarTech Leader** — Evaluates technical integration and data infrastructure; concerned with data governance and system interoperability

Secondary personas include content operations, marketing operations, demand generation leaders, and category marketing leaders.

### Q: How are personas assigned in HubSpot?

**A:** Personas are **assigned automatically via HubSpot workflow** based on job title keywords with **92.8% accuracy**.

The system matches job titles to persona definitions and populates the Persona field on contact records. If a contact is misclassified:

- Review their actual function and responsibility
- Manually override the assigned persona if needed
- Note the correction (helps improve the automation for future matches)

Use personas to tailor discovery conversations and value positioning. Different personas care about different outcomes.

### Q: What's the difference between a persona and a buying role?

**A:**

- **Persona:** _Who they are_ — Their job function and area of expertise (e.g., Content Manager, CMO, Digital Marketer)
- **Buying Role:** _What part they play in this deal_ — Their function in the sales process (e.g., Champion, Decision Maker, Technical Buyer, Blocker)

**Example:** A CMO has the persona "Marketing Leadership / CMO" and the buying role "Economic Buyer" and "Executive Sponsor" on a specific deal.

The same person can have both a persona and one or more buying roles. Personas help with targeting and messaging; buying roles help with stakeholder management and deal strategy.

### Q: What are HubSpot Buying Groups and how do I use them?

**A:** Buying Groups are HubSpot's native visual org chart for mapping the buying committee on a deal. They replaced the old contact-level "Buying Roles" property with a richer, deal-aware model. Available on Sales Hub Enterprise.

**To create one:** Open a Deal record, find the Buying Groups card in the sidebar, and click "Create a Buying Group." You can start from a **Blueprint** (pre-defined template matching Knotch's standard buying committee), auto-build from existing contacts, or assemble by hand.

**Key features:**

- Visual org chart showing who reports to whom
- Buying role tags on each contact (Champion, Economic Buyer, etc.)
- Activity heat maps (toggle sales or marketing activity to see who's engaged and who's going dark)
- Gap identification -- instantly see missing roles

**For full setup details, blueprints, and best practices, see Document 05 (Buyer Personas and Buying Committee).**

### Q: Can the same person be in multiple Buying Groups?

**A:** Yes. At Knotch, if there are two distinct buying committees at the same account (e.g., one for a Knotch One deal and another for an ACE deal), you create a **separate Buying Group for each deal**. The same contact can appear in both groups -- and may even have different roles in each (e.g., Champion on one deal, Influencer on another). This is expected and correct.

### Q: Where do I find the Buying Groups card?

**A:** It appears on Company, Contact, and Deal records. If you don't see it, ask your HubSpot admin to customize the record layout and add the Buying Groups card from the card library. You need a Sales or Service seat to create and edit buying groups.

## Data & Enrichment

### Q: Where does our contact data come from?

**A:** Knotch uses a multi-source enrichment stack:

1. **Apollo** — Phones, emails, LinkedIn URLs, and direct outbound prospecting. Apollo is the primary source for finding new contacts at target accounts and enriching phone/email coverage.
2. **Clay** — Enrichment workflows and data analysis platform. Used for advanced enrichment sequences and custom data pulls.
3. **HubSpot Breeze** — Native HubSpot enrichment; provides basic firmographics and company data
4. **AI (Internal)** — Automated persona classification (job title → persona), ICP scoring, and other AI-driven classification

**Note:** Alysio has been discontinued. Apollo and Clay are the primary enrichment sources for new data.

### Q: Why is enrichment important?

**A:** Without accurate phone numbers and email addresses, **outbound prospecting doesn't work.**

Knotch's current phone coverage is approximately 3.6% of contacts. The enrichment initiative is working to increase phone coverage to **40–60%** via Apollo enrichment, which dramatically improves:

- Outbound cold-call effectiveness
- Ability to reach buyers directly (not just email)
- Response rates on multi-channel campaigns (phone + email + LinkedIn)
- Sales cycle speed (direct conversation faster than email thread)

Every new contact added to HubSpot should be enriched with phone and email. Prioritize contacts at High Intent accounts.

## Tools & Technology

### Q: What's in our sales tech stack?

**A:**

- **HubSpot** -- CRM; single source of truth for all deal, contact, and company data (Portal 44523005)
- **Apollo** -- Prospecting and enrichment; contact discovery, phone/email coverage, outbound sequences
- **Clay** -- Advanced enrichment workflows and data analysis
- **HubSpot Breeze** -- Native HubSpot enrichment for basic firmographics
- **Otter.ai** -- Meeting recording and transcription. Records every external meeting, creates the meeting record in HubSpot, and attaches the transcript/notes automatically. This is the system of record for meeting activity.
- **Granola** -- Live AI note-taker. Runs during every meeting to capture structured notes in real time. Supplements Otter's recording. After the meeting, you have one hour to refine your notes into the meeting template. If the meeting matches a HubSpot contact, the notes are moved to a public Client Meetings folder and attached to the contact, account, and deal records in HubSpot.
- **Claude** -- AI assistant connected to Granola, HubSpot, and the rest of the stack. Ask Claude questions about your deals, accounts, meeting history, pipeline, or use it to take actions across your tools.

Sales stack is designed for efficient prospecting, account mapping, meeting intelligence, and pipeline creation at scale.

### Q: How does the meeting recording workflow work?

**A:** Every external meeting uses a two-layer recording system:

1. **Before the meeting:** Otter.ai is your primary recording tool. It joins the call automatically, records audio/video, and generates a full transcript. It also creates the meeting record in HubSpot and attaches the notes.

2. **During the meeting:** Have Granola open as your live note-taker. Granola captures structured, real-time notes that supplement the Otter transcript. Use it to tag key moments, capture action items, and note buying signals as they happen.

3. **After the meeting (within 1 hour):** Review your Granola notes and refine them into the standard meeting template. This is your window to add context, clean up notes, and ensure nothing was missed. Once finalized, if the meeting matches a HubSpot contact, the notes are automatically moved to a public Client Meetings folder and attached to the person, account, and deal records in HubSpot.

**Why the 1-hour window matters:** Notes degrade fast. Details you remember clearly right after the meeting become fuzzy by end of day. The template forces structured capture of SPICED insights, next steps, and stakeholder observations while they're fresh. After one hour, the system publishes your notes -- so treat that as a hard deadline.

### Q: What is Claude and how do I use it?

**A:** Claude is an AI assistant that is connected to Granola, HubSpot, and your other tools. Every seller has access. Use it to:

- **Ask questions:** "What did we discuss in the last Mastercard meeting?" "What's the current pipeline value for my Tier I accounts?" "Who are the contacts on the Choice Hotels deal?"
- **Prep for meetings:** "Summarize everything we know about this account" "What SPICED fields are missing on this deal?" "What did the last meeting transcript say about their decision timeline?"
- **Take actions:** "Update the next step on the EY deal to 'Send proposal by Friday'" "Add this contact to the Blackstone deal" "What are my hygiene flags this week?"
- **Analyze data:** "Which of my deals are single-threaded?" "Show me deals with no activity in the last 14 days" "What's my pipeline coverage ratio?"

Think of Claude as a research assistant, meeting prep tool, and CRM co-pilot rolled into one. The more data is in HubSpot and Granola, the more useful Claude becomes.

### Q: How do I use Apollo?

**A:** Apollo has two primary uses:

1. **Prospecting (Finding new contacts):** Search target accounts, identify decision-makers and key personas, export contacts into sequences for outbound email and calling campaigns
   - Filter by job title, company, seniority, location
   - Build lists of target personas at specific accounts
   - Use Apollo's templates for consistent outbound messaging

2. **Enrichment (Filling in data):** Enrich existing contacts in HubSpot with phone numbers, email addresses, and LinkedIn profile URLs
   - Import existing contact list
   - Apollo fills in missing data (phones, emails, LinkedIn)
   - Sync enriched data back to HubSpot

**Outbound campaigns** are designed around **four role clusters** (decision-maker personas):

- C-Suite / Executive (CMO, VP Marketing)
- Manager (Director, Senior Manager)
- Specialist (Coordinator, Manager—day-to-day)
- Influencer / Technical (Analyst, Operations)

Tailor messaging and value prop to persona. C-suite cares about ROI and strategic impact; specialists care about workflow and usability.

## Best Practices

### Q: What's the #1 thing I should do every week?

**A:** **Friday Pipeline Scrub — 15 minutes.**

1. Open the Deal Pipeline Hygiene Dashboard (Reporting > Dashboards in HubSpot)
2. Filter by your name
3. Review every hygiene flag and resolve it:
   - No Amount → Add amount
   - No Contacts → Add contact, assign buying role
   - No Recent Activity / Stalled → Log recent activity or update next step
   - Single-Threaded → Map additional contacts
   - Stale Next Steps → Schedule next step, update date
   - Past-Due Close → Update close date or close deal
4. Refresh and confirm zero flags

**Why:** This single habit eliminates 90% of deal hygiene issues, improves forecast accuracy, and makes every manager conversation more productive.

### Q: What should I do after every meaningful interaction with a prospect or customer?

**A:** You have a **1-hour window** after every meeting to complete this workflow:

**Immediately (during/right after the meeting):**

1. **Finalize your Granola notes** using the standard meeting template. Granola captured real-time notes during the meeting -- now clean them up, add context, and structure them properly. This is your most important post-meeting action. Once the hour is up, notes are published to the Client Meetings folder and attached to the contact, account, and deal in HubSpot.

2. **Otter.ai already created the meeting record** in HubSpot and attached the transcript. Verify it's on the correct deal record. If it landed on the wrong record or didn't associate, fix the association manually.

**Within the 1-hour window:**

3. **Add any new contacts** discovered and assign each a **buying role**:
   - Don't leave contacts unassigned
   - If a contact was mentioned but not yet on the deal, create them

4. **Update SPICED fields** if you learned something new:
   - Situation: refined understanding of their current state
   - Pain: confirmed specific problems they're facing
   - Impact: quantified business impact of not solving problem
   - Critical Event: identified urgency driver (budget cycle, initiative deadline)
   - Decision Criteria: learned how they evaluate vendors

5. **Update the Buying Group org chart** if reporting lines or roles became clearer:
   - Who reports to whom?
   - Who has budget authority?
   - Who are the key influencers?
   - Toggle the activity heat map to check engagement gaps

6. **Reassess and update Next Step and Next Step Date:**
   - Commit to specific next action
   - Put it in the calendar
   - Update deal record to match calendar commitment

**Pro tip:** Use Claude to speed this up. Ask it to pull the Granola notes, suggest SPICED field updates, or identify which contacts need to be added to the deal.

**Why:** HubSpot can only coach on what's in the system. Otter and Granola automate most of the logging, but the 1-hour refinement window is where you add the strategic context that makes the data actionable for coaching, forecasting, and deal strategy.

### Q: What should I check before every pipeline review with my manager?

**A:** **Before sitting down with your manager for a pipeline review, ensure:**

1. **All deal basics are current:**
   - No past-due close dates
   - Next steps are in the future (not stale)
   - Close dates match your honest forecast

2. **Zero hygiene flags on your deals**
   - Run the hygiene dashboard
   - Filter to your name
   - Resolve every flag

3. **Buyer roles assigned for all contacts on every deal:**
   - No contact should sit unassigned
   - Every contact should have a clear role (Champion, Decision Maker, etc.)
   - Builds credibility that you've done discovery

4. **Forecast categories reflect your honest assessment:**
   - Omitted: low conviction deals (early stage, unclear fit)
   - At Risk: deals with issues blocking close (single-threaded, stalled, missing stakeholder)
   - Commit: deals with strong buyer consensus and clear path to close (80%+ confidence)
   - Upside: deals you believe will close but have lower conviction (60–80%)

**Why:** This ensures reviews are productive, forecasts are credible, and you're not surprised by manager observations.

### Q: What does "if it's not in HubSpot, it didn't happen" mean?

**A:** Leadership coaches and forecasts from CRM data. If you had a conversation with a prospect but didn't log it in HubSpot:

- Your manager has no visibility into your activity
- Your pipeline metrics are incomplete
- Your forecast is unreliable
- You have no institutional memory if you leave or if another rep takes over
- Coaching and support is generic because patterns aren't visible

**Practical implication:** Log everything—calls, meetings, emails, research, competitive intel—directly on the deal record. This creates a paper trail, improves forecast accuracy, and enables managers to coach you effectively.
